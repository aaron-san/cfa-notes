Inventories

FIFO vs LIFO

  • In a period of rising prices, FIFO produces higher net income and higher ending inventory value
  • In a period of rising prices, LIFO produces lower net income and lower ending inventory value
  • LIFO conformity rule requires that if a company uses LIFO for tax purposes, it must also use LIFO for financial reporting purposes
  • LIFO liquidation occurs when a company using LIFO sells more inventory than it purchases during a period, resulting in older, lower-cost inventory being used to calculate COGS, which increases net income and taxes

Lower of Cost or Market (LCM)

  • Inventory is reported at the lower of its historical cost or its market value (replacement cost)
  • U.S. GAAP requires LCM for LIFO and retail inventory methods; IFRS requires lower of cost or net realizable value for all methods
  • IAS: subsequent reversal permitted
  • U.S. GAAP: subsequent reversal prohibited
Cost

  • Excludes: abnormal amounts, storage costs, selling costs, admin overheads
