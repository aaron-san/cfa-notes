Valuing a Target Company

  1. Discounted cash flow method

  • Determine FCFF

  • Determine discount rate (target company WACC adjusted for merger effects)

  • Discount cash flows to present

  • Determine terminal value (constant growth or market multiple)

  • Advantages:

    • Easy to model changes in cash flows from synergies
    • Might avoid biases in current market data
    • Easy to customize

  • Disadvantages:

    • Difficult to model when cash flows are negative
    • Estimation error of the terminal value
    • Can be highly sensitive to the discount rate
  1. Comparable Company Analysis

  • Look at similar firms that were aquired

  • Add a takeover premium to target's value to determine fair price

  • Advantages:

    • Easy access to data
    • Value estimates come from the market (lower estimation error)

  • Disadvantages:

    • The market might be valueing comparable firms incorrectly
    • Must determine takeover premium separately
    • Difficult to incorporate synerges or changes in capital structures
    • Historical takeover premium data might be outdated
  1. Comparables Transaction Analysis

  • Look at values of recent takeover transactions, not just comparable firms

  • No need to calculate separate takeover premium

  • Advantages:

    • No need to estimate a takeover premium
    • Estimates of value are directly derived from recent deal prices

  • Disadvantages:

    • Past M&A transactions might be mispriced
    • Possibly not enough comparable transaction data
    • Difficult to incorporate synergies or changing capital structures
